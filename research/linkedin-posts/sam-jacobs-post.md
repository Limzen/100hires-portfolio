## Content

Not nodded through in a meeting where everyone leaves with a different understanding of what just happened.

Whenever I leave a meeting thinking "I probably should have said something," I'm almost always right.

Maybe someone committed to an unrealistic timeline. Maybe an initiative doesn't have a clear owner. Maybe I'm not convinced we actually made a decision.

I can feel it while I'm sitting there. But another voice starts negotiating.
"Not now." "Let's keep the momentum." "We'll clean it up afterward."

We almost never do. Two weeks later we're having the same conversation, except now everyone remembers it differently.

Human beings can endure almost any amount of hard work when they understand why they're doing it. What they struggle to endure is uncertainty.

The kindest leaders I've worked with weren't the ones who made everyone feel good. They were the ones who made reality unmistakably clear.
